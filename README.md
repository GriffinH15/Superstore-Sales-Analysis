# Superstore-Sales-Analysis

## Overview
Analyzed 9,994 retail orders to uncover revenue trends,
regional performance, and category profitability.

## Questions Investigated
- Which region drives the most revenue and profit?
- Which product category has the best profit margin?
- How does sales volume shift month over month?

## Key Findings
- I thought it was interesting how the western region had the most sales at $725,457, since the east generally has a greater population. There must be a dedicated customer base in this region to allow it to be the highest sales region. The southern region also had the lowest sales, which maybe could be because of a lack of stores or less consumer base located in this area.

- For where money should be invested in, the profits point to technology being the highest profit and profit margins, with office supplies as a close second. I believe a majority of time and funds should be invested here as there will be a greater ROI compared to furniture. Technology profit was $145,454 while furniture was only $18,451 respectively.

- Along these lines, phones actually were the sub category that that the greatest sales, maybe due to customer demand/price level of the purchase. However, chairs actually had an extremely high level of sales, yet furniture as a whole had extremely low profit compared to fields like technology. Overall for sub categories accessories, copiers and phones had the highest profit. Phones brought in over $330,000 in sales, and chairs weren't far behind at abour $328,000, yet furniture as a whole brought in significantly less profit than technology.
